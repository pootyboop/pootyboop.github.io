Things to Find

Structures

There are a handful of structures to find across Mutablus. Some are fairly common while others are tougher to find. Here is a complete list of each structure and where to find it:

  • House - usually found near mountains. Houses are fairly common and can appear by themselves or in rare mountain cities. They only crop up in grassy areas and contain workbenches (needed to craft shovels).
  • Cave - a common small structure. Caves appear in stoney areas, typically in groups of 3 or more. Each cave has a small number of picks for you to collect.
  • Boat - an abandoned ship. They appear on the water, sometimes docked in a small pool. Boats are uncommon and always hold a map in the captain's quarters.
  • Pyramid - home of the pharoah. Pharoahs offer orbs in exchange for idols and ore. Pyramids are uncommon and appear exclusively in sandy areas.
  • Tower - the rarest structure in Mutablus. Towers offer free orbs, making them a much easier alternative to gathering ore for pharoahs. Towers are rare, appearing only in snowy areas.

Biomes

Mutablus hosts a variety of biomes. Here are some fun ones to keep an eye out for:

  • Field of Snowmen - pay attention! Some are missing their arms and could use a hand.
  • Mountain Range - like a forest, but wih big, annoying mountains instead of trees. Good luck getting through.
  • Necropolis - a desert dotted with pyramids and dig sites.
  • Stone Forest - water has started pooling in the stone here. Grass grew and caves formed.
  • Ocean - a large body of water, often hosting a boat or two.
  • True Desert - a full screen of sand. That's it.
  • Mountain City - the rarest biome in Mutablus. Mountain cities are home to loads of houses and mountains. They are extremely difficult to navigate without picks, so come prepared!

Points of Interest

These are some miscellaneous fun things that you can find in the world. Keep an eye out!

  • Dig Site - found in sandy areas. Dig sites can be excavated with a shovel to get an idol.
  • Totem - ancient, headless artifact. Place an idol atop it to be showered in tools.
  • Fleet - a bunch of boats sailing together. Where are they going?
How to Play

How to Play

Controls

  • WASD/Arrow Keys - Move, Interact
  • Space - Use Orb
  • M - Use Map

Getting Started

Mutablus is a simple exploration game. You will start in a randomly generated area possibly including trees, rivers, and snowmen. Walk off-screen in any direction to enter a new area. You will find houses, boats, pyramids, and other structures in the world. Walk into things to interact with them.

Mutablus Mutates

When you enter a new area, the previous area will transform. Forests might become slightly different forests and deserts might become oceans. Keep this in mind when entering buildings - you won't always walk out to the same area you entered from!

Items

You will notice a handful of icons on the left side of your screen. These are items you can collect and use.


Here's all you need to know about each item:

  • Log - collected by breaking a tree (just walk into it). They can also be found laying near trees. Logs allow you to cross water and, more importantly, put arms on snowmen.
  • Ore - found by mining a mountain. Pharoahs, found in pyramids, like ore and will trade you for orbs.
  • Pick - breaks cave stalagmites and mountains. Picks are collected from caves and totems. Keep in mind that they are consumable and break after a single use!
  • Shovel - excavates dig sites. These are crafted at workbenches in houses or received from totems. They are also consumable and break after a single use.
  • Map - usually reveals a hidden point of interest nearby. Maps sometimes appear in caves and houses. You can always find one on a boat. Maps don't always reveal something when used - they can be hard to decipher!
  • Orb - allows you to teleport to a new area. These are useful when you get stuck or want to quickly find something. They are obtained by trading ore to a pharoah or by finding a tower. If you don't want to collect these, you can "poor man's orb" by walking back and forth off the screen.
  • Idol - found by excavating a dig site. Pharoahs LOVE idols and will give you 10 orbs in exchange for a single idol. Alternatively, you can place an idol atop a totem for a bunch of new tools.

You've Got the Basics!

That's all you should need to get going. Once you have made some discoveries in the game for yourself, spy the Things to Find tab for more fun (and spoiler-y) stuff.

Mutablus is a top-down exploration and item management game set in an ever-changing, procedurally generated world. The world contains different biomes, structures, and items to find.

‚å® DEVELOPMENT

Mutablus was a 2nd year university project developed in JavaScript with p5.js. It uses wave function collapse to generate terrain and structures, drawing inspiration from old-school exploration games like Adventure and the Ultima series as well as procedural worldgen and its use in Oskar St√•lberg‚Äôs games like Bad North.

When creating Mutablus, I was fascinated with the Wave Function Collapse algorithm used by Oskar St√•lberg and other developers to create a procedurally generated world without the use of Perlin noise. I reverse engineered a simple version of the algorithm in p5.js.

After an initial seed-based generation which collapses all tiles to a singular possiblility, two more passes are applied to modify the environment (e.g. add structures, generate certain biomes) before a final quality pass which removes ugly isolated tiles. Seed-based "random" values are used to determine the biome, structures, some world gen factors, and other points of interest.

As the JavaScript is hosted here for the game to run, you can read my algorithm for yourself (and by extension, the individual Tile class).

The idea for a mutable world originated from swirlstorms in the Hierre system - mysterious waves that rearrange solid objects while leaving them almost entirely undamaged (like the eye of a tornado).
